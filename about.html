About Me

I’m Satyam Yadav, an Embedded Systems Engineer with a deep passion for building real-time robotic and IoT systems that solve real-world problems. With over 2 years of hands-on experience, I specialize in embedded C/C++ programming, sensor integration, PCB prototyping, and intelligent automation.

Currently pursuing a B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering (AI & Cybernetics) at VIT Bhopal, I’ve worked on several impactful projects ranging from gesture-controlled humanoid robots to field-tested agricultural bots using OpenCV for disease detection. My academic focus and project work intersect across embedded systems, robotics, signal processing, and control systems.

I’m proficient with microcontrollers like Arduino, ESP32, and STM32, and communication protocols including UART, SPI, and I2C. My workflow includes tools such as ROS, MATLAB, KiCAD, and FreeRTOS, allowing me to design and implement reliable, modular systems for real-time applications.

What drives me is the intersection of hardware and intelligence — I strive to create embedded solutions that are not only functional but also scalable, educational, and impactful. Whether it's developing robotics kits for schools or optimizing embedded firmware to reduce system latency, I approach every challenge with curiosity and precision.

Outside the lab, I’m also actively engaged in collaborative problem-solving, documentation, and leading project teams. My long-term goal is to contribute to the evolution of autonomous systems and embedded AI by creating tools and platforms that bridge technology and human needs.
